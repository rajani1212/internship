Eliciting Requirements

This phase is like the starting point of requirement analysis. It involves talking to different people like users, customers, and stakeholders to gather all the necessary information about what the product should do. Techniques such as interviews, surveys, and observations are used to understand their needs and expectations.

Importance of Requirements Elicitation

1. Compliance with Business Objectives: The process of elicitation guarantees that the software development endeavors are in harmony with the wider company aims and objectives. Comprehending the business context facilitates the development of a solution that adds value for the company.
2. User Satisfaction: It is easier to create software that fulfills end usersâ€™ needs and expectations when they are involved in the requirements elicitation process. Higher user pleasure and acceptance of the finished product are the results of this.
3. Time and Money Savings: Having precise and well-defined specifications aids in preventing miscommunication and rework during the development phase. As a result, there will be cost savings and the project will be completed on time.
