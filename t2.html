A/B Testing (Split Testing):

A/B testing is a data-driven approach to compare two versions of a product element and see which one performs better with users. It's like running a controlled experiment to determine the most effective design or feature.

  • A website or app interface element (e.g., call-to-action button, product page layout) is modified to create two variations (Version A and Version B).
  • Users are randomly divided into groups, and each group is shown one of the variations.
  • User engagement metrics like clicks, conversions, or time spent on a page are tracked for both versions.
  • Based on the data, the version that performs better in terms of user engagement is chosen for implementation.

Example:

An e-commerce website wants to increase sign-ups for their newsletter. They create two versions of their signup form:

  • Version A: Simple form with email address field and a signup button.
  • Version B: Same form with an additional checkbox for receiving promotional offers.

Through A/B testing, they can see which version yields a higher signup rate, helping them optimize their signup process.
